Why Nebraska Homes Need This

🌡️
140°F Attic Summers
On a hot summer afternoon, your attic can reach 140°F while you're trying to keep the living space at 75°F. That's a 65-degree battle your air conditioner fights nonstop — and loses without proper insulation.
❄️
Sub-Zero Winter Losses
Nebraska winters regularly push below 0°F. Heat rises — and without a proper thermal barrier at your attic floor, your furnace is essentially heating the outdoors. Every degree of R-value you add is money staying in your home.
📋
Nebraska Code Requires It
Nebraska's 2018 energy code sets R-49 as the minimum for new construction. The 2021 code raises this to R-60 — the level state researchers identified as having the largest economic potential of all insulation upgrades studied.

The Air Sealing Multiplier

Insulation slows the transfer of heat through your attic floor. But if air is bypassing that insulation through gaps around light fixtures, pipes, wiring, and framing — you're leaving most of the money on the table.

Air sealing closes those bypasses. A large-scale analysis of 23,000 before-and-after retrofit measurements found that professional air sealing consistently reduces air infiltration by 20–30%, with a median result of exactly 25%. Combined with upgraded insulation, this is what delivers the 15–25% HVAC savings documented by the EPA's ENERGY STAR program.
